Duende Dominicano

Yaroa

Yaroa is a popular Dominican street food that’s both savory and filling. It’s typically made by layering French fries or fried plantains as the base, then topping them with seasoned meats such as shredded chicken, ground beef, or pork. Over the meat, generous amounts of cheese are melted, and it’s all finished with drizzles of ketchup, mayonnaise, and sometimes mustard. The dish is known for its rich, comforting flavors and is often enjoyed late at night, making it a favorite comfort food in the Dominican Republic

Ingredients

  • French fries or mashed ripe plantains
  • Meat: ground beef, pork, chicken, or a mix
  • Tomato sauce (or a tomato-based sauce)
  • Onions, garlic (sautéed)
  • Bell pepper (in some versions)
  • Cheese (usually melty cheeses like cheddar, mozzarella, or blends)
  • Condiments / toppings: a “pink sauce” (mayo + ketchup or mayonnaise + ketchup + sometimes mustard), sometimes hot sauce or chimi sauce, fresh herbs (cilantro)
  • Seasonings / extra flavorings: sazón, sofrito, salt, pepper, oregano, etc...

instructions

  1. If using French fries: Peel (optional) and cut potatoes into sticks, rinse, and pat dry. Fry in hot oil until golden and crispy. Drain and season with salt. (If using frozen fries, cook as package directs.) Or If using mashed ripe plantains (or yuca): Peel plantains (or yuca) and cut into chunks. Boil in salted water until tender. Mash with a little butter and a splash of the cooking water until smooth.
  2. Cook the Meat: Heat 1–2 table spoon oil in a skillet. Add onion, garlic, and bell pepper; sauté until soft. Stir in ground meat and cook until browned. Add tomato sauce, sazón, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer 5–7 minutes until saucy.
  3. Assemble the Yaroa In a baking dish (or foil tray): Spread the base layer (fries or mashed plantains/yuca). Add the seasoned meat on top. Sprinkle with shredded cheese.
  4. Melt the Cheese Place the dish under a broiler or in a hot oven for 3–5 minutes, just until the cheese melts and bubbles.
  5. Drizzle mayo and ketchup (or pink sauce) on top. Add mustard or fresh cilantro if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!
